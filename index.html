How it works

Your part takes five minutes.

Three steps. Only one of them is yours.

  1. You send one email. We write it and email it to you. Pick three clients, forward it, and you’re done. Five minutes.
  2. We interview each client. The email has a link, so they pick a 15-minute slot that suits them. We ask about the problem, why they chose you, what it was like working together, and what’s different now.
  3. We write everything up. You review it. Nothing is published. Nothing is sent back to your client. Not until you approve every word.

The bit you’re actually worried about

Will this annoy my client?

It’s the real reason most people never get round to this. It isn’t the writing. It’s the thought of a stranger ringing up the client you spent three years earning.

So here is exactly what happens to them.

  • We never email your client. You do. We write it and send it to you. You forward it, from your own address, to the three people you choose. We don’t hold their details and we can’t contact them.
  • They come to us, or they don’t. Inside the email is a link to book a slot. We only ever speak to the clients who choose to pick up the phone.
  • It’s 15 minutes, and we’re not selling them anything. No pitch, no upsell, no adding them to a list. We ask about a project that went well and then we go away.
  • If they don’t book, that’s the end of it. There’s nobody to chase, because we can’t. No reminders, no follow-ups. We interview someone else instead, at no extra cost.
  • Nothing about it is a surprise. They agree to the call knowing it’s for a case study you’ll publish. So when it goes up, it’s their own words — not a version of them we invented.
