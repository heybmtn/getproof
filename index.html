Why it works

What's the difference between a case study and a testimonial? Structure and length. A testimonial is a nice line. A case study is the whole story — the challenge, the choice, the result — and that's what actually sells.

That's why the best ones aren't written from assumptions — they're built from real conversations. A short interview uncovers the details that make your work credible: the challenge your client faced, why they chose you, what changed, and the results they achieved.

Those stories become powerful sales assets your team can use across your website, proposals, presentations and marketing.
